Being an effective manager is hard. You have to maintain your air of superiority, while also ensuring that your underlings harbor the correct levels of fear and adoration in their hearts. You have to seem to be engaged and on top of everything, while also being sure to not actually contribute any effort to the projects under your purview. While this balancing act may sound impossible, the tips below will have you getting it right in no time.

## 1. Be late to every meeting

There’s nothing more beneath a manager’s station than punctuality. The later you are, the more you lessers will come to appreciate the magnitude of your greatness. It will also give them some time to chat amongst themselves and get the meeting’s five minutes of actual productivity out of the way before you arrive.

## 2. Insist that every decision be made by consensus

If you come out and say what you think about something, there’s a non-zero chance that you’ll be blamed when something goes wrong. Instead, frame every decision as a discussion, and use your superior facilitation skills to squash ideas that you don’t like and spin others to match what you want to happen. This way, you get things the way you want them, but take on no responsibility. You also preserve the opportunity to act as though you knew the decision was wrong all along, if it turns out bad.

## 3. Get others to do your work by bringing it to a meeting “working on it together”

Have a task you can’t delegate without making it obvious you’re just avoiding work? Schedule a meeting to “brainstorm” on the topic. You can make the attendees do almost all of it, and they’ll be flattered that someone as important as you wants their input. Plus, there’s no better team building activity than collaborative editing.

## 4. If people don’t like your idea just keep bringing it up

Have you ever had an idea rejected because it’s “too hard to implement” or “makes the process too complicated” or “only benefits you and would take too many development hours?” A truly great manager wouldn’t let that get in the way. If your developers tell you they can’t do it, just being it up again at the next meeting and pretend you don’t remember their reasons. Eventually they’ll get so sick of explaining, they’ll agree to do it just to shut you up. Managing accomplished.

## 5. If you make a mistake, defensibly blame it on mysterious technical problems

A good manager never makes a typo. Instead, their messages get inexplicably altered by E-mail servers. No one will respect you if they see you as human.

## 6. Respond to every E-mail thread, whether you have something to say or not

A great manager is involved in every discussion, even when there’s nothing to be said. If you don’t know what else to say, just reiterate whatever the last person said, prefaced with, “just to confirm…”

## 7. Always complain about being busy

Even if you literally have nothing to do, tell everyone how busy you are at every opportunity. The busier you are, the more respect you get. The more you compassion about it, the more sympathy you earn.
